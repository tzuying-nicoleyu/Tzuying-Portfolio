    • BCG Churning Analysis

    This is a customer churn analysis of Boston Consulting Group (BCG) on Forage Job Sim. The data is based on BCG's client - PowerCo, a energey provider for small to medium-sized enterprises, and it is encoded to ensure data privacy. The analysis provides insights into the factors that contribute to customer churn, constructs a random forest model with 85% of accuracy rate in predicting customer churn, and suggests strategies to reduce customer churn.

    Cost Efficient Classifications for Predicting Credit Default

    This is a project that choosing the most cost efficient classification model for predicting credit default. We evaluated models including logistic regression, decision tree, naive bayes, and random forest. The data is from the Home Credit, an financial services company in Russia, and it contains 307,511 records and 122 features. The project aims to find the most cost-efficient classification model for predicting credit default.

    Cookhouse X Argus - Consulting Proposal

    This is client engagement proposal for insurance company Argus partnering with Cookhouse Design Thinking Bootcamp. The projects aimed to increase funds in Argus with sources other than client's pesnion funds. In a team of 4, we suggested a new services tiers structure to increase the volume of funds and a new marketing strategy to increase customer engagement and loyalty.

    Mainstay Housing Aging in Place Report

    This project demonstrated the work as Research Assistant in Mainstay Housing's Age-in-Place research. The project focused on analyzing survey data from Mainstay Housing's agents to better understand the current circumstances of aging in social housing and finding corresponding goverment policies and resources that improving aging in place.

    2021 ASA DataFest Case Competition

    This was a case competition that I participated in 2021 ASA DataFest. Due to data privacy and security reasons, I could not share the data and code. However, I could share the final report. The case was about analyzing survey data of non-medical use of prescription drugs from more than 10,000 respondents. The final report focused on the exploratory data analysis and finding patterns of misusing prescription drugs
